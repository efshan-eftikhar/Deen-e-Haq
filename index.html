Emaan-e-Mufassal

آمَنتُ بِاللّٰہِ وَمَلٰئِکَتِہٖ وَکُتُبِہٖ وَرُسُلِہٖ وَالْیَوْمِ الْاٰخِرِ وَالْقَدْرِ خَیْرِہٖ وَشَرِّہٖ مِنَ اللّٰہِ تَعَالٰی وَالْبَعْثِ بَعْدَ الْمَوْتِ۔

Translation: I believe in Allah, His angels, His books, His messengers, the Last Day, and in the fact that everything good or bad is decided by Allah Almighty, and in life after death.

Emaan-e-Mujmal

آمَنتُ بِاللّٰہِ کَمَا ہُوَ بِأَسْمَائِہٖ وَصِفَاتِہٖ وَقَبِلْتُ جَمِیْعَ أَحْکَامِہٖ۔

Translation: I believe in Allah as He is with His names and attributes, and I accept all of His commands.

Pillars of Islam

1. Shahadah (Faith)

Declaration that there is no god but Allah and Muhammad (ﷺ) is His Messenger. (Qur'an 3:18)

2. Salah (Prayer)

Five daily prayers are obligatory upon Muslims. (Qur'an 2:43)

3. Sawm (Fasting)

Fasting during the month of Ramadan is mandatory. (Qur'an 2:183)

4. Zakat (Charity)

Giving a portion of wealth to those in need is a religious duty. (Qur'an 9:60)

5. Hajj (Pilgrimage)

Every Muslim must perform Hajj once in their lifetime if they are able. (Qur'an 3:97)

6. Jihad

Jihad, in its true sense, means striving or struggling in the path of Allah. It includes spiritual and physical efforts to improve one’s faith and community. The Quran states: "And strive for Allah with the striving due to Him." (Quran: Surah Al-Hajj 22:78)

Core Islamic Values

Sabr (Patience): "And be patient, for indeed, Allah is with the patient." (Qur'an 2:153)
Shukar (Gratitude): "If you are grateful, I will surely increase your favor upon you." (Qur'an 14:7)
Insaniyat (Humanity): "And lower to them the wing of humility out of mercy." (Qur'an 17:24)
Adl (Justice): "Indeed, Allah commands you to render trusts to whom they are due and judge with justice." (Qur'an 4:58)
Ihsaan (Excellence): "Indeed, Allah loves those who do good." (Qur'an 2:195)
Tawbah (Repentance): "And turn to Allah in repentance, all of you, O believers." (Qur'an 24:31)
